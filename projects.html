Business

LukeHacksLife LLC

My personal brand and consulting practice, based out of Fairfax, VA. I started this because I kept getting asked the same question: "How do I get more people to notice what I'm building?" And I realized I had a real answer — built from years of trial, error, and honest study.

I work with solopreneurs and small teams on brand clarity, content strategy, and the kind of marketing that doesn't make you feel like a salesperson. My goal is to help people build audiences without losing who they are in the process.

The name comes from a simple belief: you can hack the process of building a life you love. Not shortcuts — just smarter approaches and honest effort.

Content

YouTube Channel

Long-form videos about faith, personal development, and the unglamorous side of building something on your own. No production crew, no studio — just a camera, good lighting, and an honest take.

I started the channel because I was tired of business content that only showed the wins. My most-watched videos tend to be the ones where I admit something didn't work. There's an audience for real.

My goal is to publish consistently and build the channel as a place people come when they need to feel less alone in the grind.

A passion project I started to document my marathon training — but it became something else. It's really about what the long race teaches you: patience, grit, showing up when you don't feel like it, and finding meaning in something that has no shortcut.

I write race reports, training reflections, and occasional pieces on how distance running has reshaped my faith and how I think about building a business. It's niche, and I love that.
